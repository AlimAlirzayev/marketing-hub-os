# Campaign brief — Travel Insurance · Baku–Tbilisi

## Strategic premise

From **1 January 2026**, travel insurance is **mandatory** for entry to Georgia.
Xalq Sigorta is positioning as the **default, calm, authoritative choice** for
this new requirement. Not the cheapest, not the flashiest — the one a senior
manager picks without thinking.

## Audience

- **Primary:** 28–45 year old Azerbaijani professionals who travel
  cross-border 2–6× a year. Mostly business + family.
- **Secondary:** First-time Georgia visitors (post-Jan 2026 awareness wave).

## Voice rules (carried over from `brand_kit/brand.md`)

- Calm authority. No exclamation marks. No clickbait.
- Short declarative sentences in Azerbaijani.
- The image's job is to make the deadline feel **handled**, not stressful.

## Visual brand atmosphere

- Mood: **restrained premium**, like a private banking ad.
- Subjects: confident travelers, not anxious tourists.
- Red is the brand atmosphere (suitcase + Xalq Sigorta footer) — never a wash.
- Caucasus geography is the implicit story; the bridge anchors the route.

## What must always be true

- Negative space: upper-left 1/3 for headline, bottom 180px for footer.
- One couple only.
- Red suitcase: lower-left foreground, edge-cropped.
- Subtle shield reflection on window glass: second-glance discoverable.
- Subjects looking AT THE VIEW, not at the camera.

## What we measure

- Engagement: comment + share rate vs. last quarter's travel-insurance posts.
- Conversion: clicks to xalqsigorta.az/travel + 183 hotline volume.
- Brand health: perceived "trust" score in audience pulse survey.
