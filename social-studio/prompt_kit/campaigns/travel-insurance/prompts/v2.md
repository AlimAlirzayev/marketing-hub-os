# v2 — master-level structured prompt

Built on `master_template.md`. Every layer specified, no fillers.
Tested on GPT Image 2 (via Codex CLI). Iteration log in `notes.md`.

```
=== HARD CONSTRAINTS ===
Output: ONE photographic background. 4:5 vertical, 1080×1350.
This is a background plate only. The headline, body copy, brand logo,
contact lockup, and legal microcopy will be overlaid in post-production -
do NOT render any of them, NOT EVEN as decorative shapes.
Subject count: exactly one couple in frame. No bystanders, no crew.
Scene anchor: interior of a modern intercity passenger train.

=== CAMERA & LENS ===
Equivalent focal length: 50mm full-frame.
Aperture: f/2.8 (subject-sharp, background gently soft).
ISO 200 (subtle natural grain).
Subject distance: medium shot, waist-up framing.
Camera angle: slight low angle, approximately at chair-armrest height.
Framing: subjects right of center, window left of subjects.

=== LIGHTING ===
Key light: soft window light from camera right. Late morning warmth,
color temperature ~4800K. Light source is the window itself - directional,
diffused by glass.
Fill: ambient cabin reflection at 1:4 ratio. Faces lit but with retained
shadow modeling on the off-side cheek and jaw.
Catchlights: small, soft, visible in both subjects' eyes.
No artificial spot lights. No HDR look. No backlit halo.

=== SUBJECT IDENTITY ===
Both subjects: Caucasus-Azerbaijani features, ages 28-34. Confident,
relaxed, NOT smiling teeth-out - quiet contentment, lips closed or
faintly parted.
Woman: shoulder-length dark brown hair, minimal makeup (natural lip,
subtle eye), small gold stud earrings. Cream or pale-beige knit sweater,
NO patterns, NO logos. Seated closer to the window.
Man: short well-groomed beard, dark hair, plain charcoal henley or
unbranded sweatshirt. Seated beside the woman, slightly behind her line.
Both NOT looking at the camera. Their gaze tracks the railway bridge
or mountain view through the window.
Body language: relaxed, leaning slightly toward the view. Seasoned
travelers, not first-timers. No tension, no posed positioning.

=== BRAND PROPS ===
Red hard-shell suitcase: positioned in the LOWER-LEFT foreground, with
roughly 30% of the suitcase cropped out of the frame. Color must be a
clean strong red matching HEX #E31E24 (Xalq Sigorta brand red).
Surface: matte, modern, no scuffs, no visible brand name. Reading-side
handle stowed.
Smartphone: in the woman's hand, screen angled toward the camera so the
display is partially visible. Screen content: an abstract red shield
silhouette and three short horizontal data bars on a clean white
background. ZERO readable letters, NO numbers, NO logo on screen.
Passports: in the man's hand, held casually. Navy-blue covers (Azerbaijan
style), slight leather wear, ONE folded paper ticket peeking out the top.

=== SCENE BACKGROUND ===
Outside the window: a real Caucasus-style railway moment. The Baku-Tbilisi
route's signature visual: a steel railway viaduct OR mountain cutting,
mid-distance. Daylight is hazy and warm but not postcard-saturated.
Mountains in the far background, soft atmospheric perspective. Power-line
poles allowed if subtle. No towns visible.
Train interior: modern Stadler/CAF style aluminum trim, dark navy fabric
on seats, soft tray-table edge in frame. A small unlit reading lamp above
the woman, decorative only.

=== BRAND ATMOSPHERE ===
At the far-LEFT edge of the frame (roughly the leftmost 20% width), a
soft burgundy atmospheric fade. Color #5C0F12 transitioning to fully
transparent toward the center. This is NOT a wash, NOT a band, NOT a
solid stripe — it is a feathered atmospheric haze. The viewer should
not consciously notice it.
On the window glass, centered roughly over the railway-bridge view:
a transparent reflection of a simple shield outline. Soft edges,
opacity approximately 12%, width approximately 120 pixels. The shield
must be a SECOND-GLANCE discovery, not a focal point. No glow, no
fantasy effect.

=== NEGATIVE SPACE FOR OVERLAY ===
Upper-LEFT third of the frame (top 480px × left 540px): calm, low-
information area. The deepest dark in the gradient lives here. No
faces, no props, no detail competing with future headline typography.
Bottom 180px of the frame: clean footer zone. No subject body parts,
no suitcase handles, no phone. May contain blurred floor or seat edge
only.

=== STYLE ANCHOR ===
Visual reference: Mont Blanc 2024 travel campaign, Hermès "Petit h"
documentary series, Magnum Photos travel-editorial work. Aesthetic is
editorial documentary photography, NOT lifestyle stock photography.
NOT Getty, NOT iStock, NOT Shutterstock visual language.

=== QUALITY DIRECTIVES ===
Visible skin texture - pores, faint freckles allowed. Natural hand
anatomy: all 10 fingers correct, knuckles defined, no waxy webbing.
Fabric weave visible on close inspection. Film grain at ISO 200
equivalent - subtle, organic, NOT digital noise.
No painterly smearing, no AI-plastic skin, no warped suitcase wheels,
no impossible reflections, no extra fingers, no double pupils.

=== EXCLUSION LIST (ordered by likelihood) ===
✗ Any visible text or readable signage (NO fake brand names, NO
  fake ad copy, NO numbers, NO letters on the smartphone screen,
  NO writing on the suitcase, NO destination boards)
✗ Watermarks of any kind (Shutterstock, Getty, Pollinations, etc.)
✗ Plastic AI skin or smooth-airbrushed faces
✗ Both subjects looking at the camera (kills editorial mood)
✗ Smiling with visible teeth
✗ Heavy red wash across the entire frame
✗ Multiple couples or background passengers
✗ Floating CGI shields, fantasy effects, sci-fi glow
✗ Cartoon stylization, anime features
✗ Distorted suitcase wheels or handles
✗ Extra fingers, fused fingers, or warped hand anatomy
✗ Visible camera operator reflection in window
```

## Why this works better than v1

- **Subject identity is locked.** v1's "stylish young couple" produced
  drift across runs. v2 names hair length, color, clothing colors,
  posture, gaze direction — the model has nothing to improvise.
- **Camera/lens grounds the photo.** 50mm f/2.8 is a familiar
  photographic recipe; models trained on EXIF-tagged data respond.
- **Lighting kills the AI tell.** Naming Kelvin temperature + key/fill
  ratio + catchlight position is the difference between AI render and
  ad photography.
- **Brand props specified by HEX, not name.** Models often shift red
  toward orange-red or maroon. HEX anchors it.
- **Style anchor pivots away from stock.** "Mont Blanc / Hermès"
  triggers different latent space neighborhoods than "premium ad".
- **Exclusion list is ordered.** Models attend to early items more
  carefully. We put the most-likely failures first (fake text).
