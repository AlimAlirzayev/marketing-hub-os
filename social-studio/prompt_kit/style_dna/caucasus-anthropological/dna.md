# Style DNA — `caucasus-anthropological`

Rooted. The subject is one element among many in a place that has been
there longer than the brand. Used when the campaign needs to feel
*regional* — Azerbaijani, South Caucasus, tied to landscape and heritage.

## Lineage

- **Steve McCurry** Central Asia and Caucasus body of work.
- **Sebastião Salgado** anthropological essays — humans in landscape.
- **Anastasia Taylor-Lind** "Maidan Portraits" and Tbilisi series — modern
  subjects in heritage settings.
- **Yuri Kozyrev** documentary commissions across the region.
- **National Geographic** Caucasus-region cover photography.

Avoid: any "exotic East" tropes, postcard saturation, tourist-gaze framing.

## Subject treatment

- **Environmental portrait** — subject occupies maybe 30-50% of the frame;
  the rest is the place.
- **Pose:** Active in the environment. Walking a stone path, leaning
  against a balcony, sitting on a copper pot. The subject *belongs* there.
- **Gaze:** Often on the place itself — looking at mountains, at a doorway,
  at a craft in progress. Sometimes at the camera with measured directness
  (never a smile, never a tourist's gaze).
- **Wardrobe:** Modern but uncomplicated. Cotton, wool, leather — pieces
  that could have been worn 30 years ago AND today. No fast-fashion
  branding, no athleisure.
- **Skin tone treatment:** Honors the actual range of Caucasian-Azerbaijani
  features. Olive-warm, never pale-Northern-European-default.

## Composition

- **Subject + setting in equal partnership.** Wide enough to read context.
- **Strong leading lines** from architecture — stone walls, balcony rails,
  arched doorways, dirt roads, railway tracks.
- **Foreground anchored** in place: cobblestones, dried herbs, copper
  vessels, kilim edge, a chai glass.
- **Mid-distance focus** with environmental sharpness — the place is
  legible, not a blur.
- **Sky/landscape** carries 1/3 to 1/2 of the frame when outdoors.

## Light

- **Golden hour (low warm light)** OR overcast soft-diffused.
- **Color temperature:** 2800-3500K for golden hour; 5500K for overcast.
- **Raking light** when possible — long shadows revealing stone texture,
  fabric weave, plaster surface.
- **Mid-morning or late afternoon**, never harsh midday.

## Color story

- **Dominant palette:** Earth — terracotta, ochre, slate blue, cream stone,
  weathered copper, deep burgundy.
- **Greens rare and saturated when present** (a single patch of grass,
  a tea leaf, a herb bundle).
- **Brand red `#E31E24` naturalizes** into the palette — a clay pot, a
  rug edge, a brand prop that *feels* indigenous to the place.
- **No cool whites, no pure black** — the world is warm-shifted earth.

## Material rendering

- **Stone, plaster, copper, wool, kilim, leather, oak, brass.** Each
  material rendered with patina visible.
- **Weather and time visible** on every surface — moss, water-staining,
  hand-polish from years of use.
- **Hand-craft language:** Tool marks, slight imperfection, asymmetry.

## Setting cues (Azerbaijani specificity)

- **Architecture:** Şirvanşahlar Palace masonry, Old City Baku stone,
  carved wooden balconies, traditional doors with iron knockers.
- **Landscape:** Caspian Sea horizon line, Greater Caucasus foothills,
  Şəki tea plantations, Qobustan rock fields.
- **Objects:** Çaynik, armudu tea glass, kilim runners, copper trays,
  pomegranates, dried herbs hanging.
- **Avoid:** Western European architectural cues that don't belong here.

## Mood vocabulary

- **Pace:** Generational. The subject moves through the place the way
  the place moves through time — slowly, with weight.
- **Intimacy distance:** Anthropological. The camera observes from
  measured distance, respecting the subject's place in the world.
- **Emotional register:** Rooted, proud, present.
- **Story register:** A continuation. The subject is part of a longer
  story before and after this moment.

## Lens & camera

- **Focal length:** 35mm equivalent for environmental, 50mm for portrait.
- **Aperture:** f/4 to f/5.6 — environment legible.
- **ISO:** 200-400 with documentary grain.
- **Camera angle:** Eye-level mostly, occasional slight low to honor the
  place.

## Style-specific exclusions

```
✗ "Exotic East" tropes — veils, beggars, kitsch
✗ Tourist-gaze framing (subject as spectacle, not as inhabitant)
✗ Postcard color saturation
✗ Cool color temperatures or pure whites
✗ Modern fast-fashion branding visible
✗ Architectural cues that don't belong to the South Caucasus
✗ Glossy or "new" materials — patina is the language
✗ Studio lighting — only natural light
✗ Teeth-out smiles or any "Instagram tourist" expressions
```

## When to choose this style

- Heritage-tied campaigns (national days, traditional product
  intersections, regional pride moments)
- Travel campaigns that emphasize the *destination* over the journey
- Sustainability and longevity messaging (insurance products positioned
  as multi-generational)
- Property insurance for traditional homes
- Anything that pairs Xalq Sigorta with Azerbaijani specificity

**NOT for:** Topical news with urgency, modern lifestyle products,
younger demographic targeting.
