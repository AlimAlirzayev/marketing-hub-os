# Style DNA — `emerging-ai-luxury`

The next thing. Slightly destabilizing. The viewer can tell the image was
made by a system, and the brand wants them to know — because the brand
is the kind of brand that uses systems. Used for younger sub-products,
Instagram Reels covers, anything that should signal *current*.

## Lineage

- **Coperni 2024** runway photography — surreal-real lighting, plastic-glass
  material rendering.
- **Le Specs 2025** campaigns — composite-perfect faces with intentional
  anachronistic styling.
- **Rabanne post-AI** campaigns where the AI signature is part of the
  brand voice.
- **Mowalola Ogunlesi** for Adidas — hyper-color, hyper-clean.
- **Recent Vogue Italia** AI-assisted editorial.

Avoid: any documentary realism, any film-grain authenticity language,
any "real moment" pretense. This voice owns its constructed-ness.

## Subject treatment

- **Subject self-aware** that the medium is constructed. Slight surreal-real
  edge.
- **Pose:** Composed, near-symmetric, slightly cinematic stretch. The
  subject could be a statue or could be alive — the ambiguity is the point.
- **Faces:** Composite-perfect with intentional small "tells" — long
  fingers, slightly elongated jaw, dreamy eye shape, anachronistic styling.
  The viewer registers "this is beautiful AND not quite real."
- **Gaze:** Directly at the camera with a measured, almost confrontational
  calm. The subject sees the viewer.
- **Wardrobe:** Modern luxury with one architectural piece — a structured
  shoulder, a glass-pearl earring, a chrome belt buckle.
- **Number:** Solo strongly preferred — this voice is about the
  individual-as-archetype.

## Composition

- **Dead-center** is often correct — the symmetry signals constructed-ness.
- **Cinematic stretch** — frame feels slightly compressed horizontally,
  like an anamorphic lens.
- **Background:** Either impossibly clean (single tone, no detail) or
  impossibly chaotic (CGI fractal, layered glass).
- **Foreground depth:** Often a glass surface, a water layer, or a
  light haze the subject is *behind*.

## Light

- **Hyperreal lighting.** Too perfect to be natural, too natural to be
  CGI. Often overhead with no clear shadow direction.
- **Color temperature:** Variable — either monochromatic cool (5500-6500K)
  or saturated colored gels (sodium amber, magenta, electric teal).
- **Highlights specular and intentional** — the subject's cheekbone catches
  a single point of perfect light.
- **No shadow modeling** in the traditional sense — the face is flatly
  rendered like a painting.

## Color story

- **Two modes — pick one per campaign:**
  - **Pearl monochrome:** Oyster white, fog gray, champagne, mother-of-pearl.
    A near-monochromatic luxurious haze.
  - **Jewel saturation:** Sapphire, emerald, ruby, amethyst. Single hue
    dominant per frame.
- **Brand red `#E31E24` shifts** — either pulled toward pure cinnabar (more
  orange) for the jewel mode or muted to oxblood `#5C0F12` for the pearl
  mode.

## Material rendering

- **Modern materials photographed like baroque painting.** Plastics,
  silicones, polished glass, technical fabric — all rendered with the
  weight of marble.
- **Skin sometimes plasticized intentionally.** The poreless rendering
  is part of the voice, not a failure.
- **Hard surfaces dominate.** Soft materials read as architectural.
- **Reflections layered** — glass over glass, water over chrome.

## Mood vocabulary

- **Pace:** Suspended-eternal. The frame could last forever.
- **Intimacy distance:** Confrontational-calm. The viewer is held at
  arm's length.
- **Emotional register:** Cool. Self-possessed. Slightly haunted.
- **Story register:** No before, no after. Pure present.

## Lens & camera

- **Focal length:** 85mm or longer for compression.
- **Aperture:** f/1.4 to f/2.0 — very shallow.
- **ISO:** 50-100 — zero grain.
- **Camera angle:** Slight low, near-eye-level, never high.

## Style-specific exclusions

```
✗ Documentary realism, film grain, candid moments
✗ Subject mid-motion — this voice is suspended
✗ Earth-tone palettes (sage, ochre, terracotta)
✗ Window light or natural daylight as primary
✗ Subjects looking off-frame (this voice meets the viewer's gaze)
✗ Multiple subjects (solo only)
✗ Patina, wear, weathering on materials
✗ Asymmetric compositions
✗ "Lived-in" environments
```

## When to choose this style

- Younger sub-brand launches
- Instagram Reels cover frames
- Limited-edition or seasonal products that need to feel current
- Collaborations with art/fashion brands
- Anything explicitly addressed to a 22-30 demographic

**NOT for:** Heritage messaging, B2B, anything that needs to feel trusted
or institutional, anything regional-specific.

## Compatibility with Xalq Sigorta brand

This is the **furthest** style from Xalq Sigorta's `editorial-documentary`
default. Use it sparingly, intentionally — for example:
- A "Younger Xalq Sigorta" product line (Insurance for Gen Z / first-job
  segment)
- A 1-year campaign moment that intentionally breaks tone
- Cross-pollination collaborations (a tech partner, a streetwear brand)

NOT for: travel insurance, family insurance, property insurance, anything
core-brand.
