# Style DNA — `financial-restraint`

Calm authority. The room is quiet because the work is serious. Used when
the brand needs to feel institutional, durable, trusted — investor
communications, B2B partnerships, year-end reports translated to social.

## Lineage

- **Goldman Sachs** annual reports 2018-2024 (visual identity of the print
  reports translated to digital).
- **JPMorgan "Make More"** 2023 campaign — single subject, large negative
  space, premium materials in foreground.
- **Allianz** heritage advertising — single key light, fountain pen on
  cream paper.
- **The Economist** photographic essays — restrained subjects,
  environmental.
- **Bloomberg Markets** magazine photography.

Avoid: anything that signals startup, casual, vibrant, youth-coded.

## Subject treatment

- **Pose:** Stillness as authority. Subject seated at a desk, standing in a
  considered space, walking a measured pace. Never mid-motion.
- **Gaze:** On a document, on a partner, on the middle distance. Or
  directly at the camera but with measured restraint (never a smile).
- **Number:** Strongly prefer single subject. Two-subject only when the
  campaign requires partnership signaling.
- **Scale:** Medium to wide — read the room. The environment is part of
  the message.
- **Posture:** Upright but relaxed. Suit shoulders straight. No leaning,
  no slouching, no casual props.
- **Mouth:** Closed. Composed. Maybe the trace of consideration.

## Composition

- **Centered or symmetric** more often than off-center. Authority is
  signaled through compositional weight.
- **Large empty foreground:** Polished table, blank page, marble floor,
  open ledger. The empty space says the work is private and unhurried.
- **Subject in upper third** for vertical formats — looks down upon the
  empty workspace.
- **Architectural frames:** Doorway, window, column. The institution is
  in the frame.
- **Negative space sparse but deep** — not empty, but full of the
  *absence* of clutter.

## Light

- **Window light treated like a portrait studio softbox:** large, soft,
  directional. North-facing windows ideal.
- **Color temperature:** 4500-5500K. Daylight neutral. Not warm, not cool.
- **Crisp shadows on subject's off-side** — modeling preserved, never
  flatly even. The shadow itself is a piece of typography.
- **Skin retention:** Full texture. Adults look like adults.
- **Reflections in materials:** Brushed metal pen body, polished desk
  surface, glass paperweight. Each reflection considered.

## Color story

- **Dominant palette:** Inky charcoal, deep navy, single-malt amber,
  paper white, brushed silver.
- **Accent:** Brand red used surgically — a book spine, a pen cap, a
  ribbon on a folder, a chair back in shallow background. Never larger
  than 5% of frame.
- **Saturation register:** Very low. The image reads almost monochromatic
  before you notice the accent.
- **Texture-color blend:** Premium materials carry their own color story —
  wood grain, paper fiber, fabric weft.

## Material rendering

- **Premium materials, every one of them:** Solid wood (not veneer),
  brushed metal (not chrome), fountain pen ink on cream cotton paper,
  full-grain leather with patina, wool worsted suit cloth.
- **Touch of patina, never new-out-of-box.** The pen has been used. The
  desk has been worked at. The chair has memory.
- **Fingerprint and wear allowed and welcomed.**

## Mood vocabulary

- **Pace:** Slow but purposeful. The pace of a meeting that matters.
- **Intimacy distance:** Formal but human. The viewer is in the room as
  a respected colleague, not as a spectator.
- **Emotional register:** Considered, present, weighty.
- **Story register:** A moment of considered work. Outside the frame:
  consequence.

## Lens & camera

- **Focal length:** 35mm or 50mm for portrait, 24-35mm for environmental.
- **Aperture:** f/4 to f/5.6 — sharp through subject and environment. NOT
  shallow.
- **ISO:** 100-200, near-zero grain.
- **Camera angle:** Eye-level, never high or low. Architectural integrity.

## Style-specific exclusions

```
✗ Anything that signals startup energy (sneakers, casual postures,
   neon, vibrant)
✗ Smiles with teeth, or any "lifestyle catalog" expressions
✗ Mid-motion subjects
✗ Brand red as larger than 5% of the frame
✗ Bokeh-heavy backgrounds (this voice keeps everything legible)
✗ Three or more subjects
✗ Cluttered or busy environments
✗ Daylight that reads as "vacation" — this is work
```

## When to choose this style

- B2B campaigns and partnership announcements
- Year-end reports translated to social
- Investor-facing content
- Senior product launches (life insurance over a million AZN coverage,
  premium private-banking, corporate fleet insurance)
- LinkedIn over Instagram

**NOT for:** Travel, family, retail, anything seasonal or topical-news.
