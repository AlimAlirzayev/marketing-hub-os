# Xalq Sigorta — copywriting voice

Extension of [`social-studio/brand_kit/brand.md`](../../social-studio/brand_kit/brand.md)
with copy-specific rules. The `brand_kit` is the contract; this file
operationalizes the voice for headlines, captions, scripts.

## One-line identity

**Calm authority.** The brand that makes the deadline feel handled, not
stressful. The senior at the table whose silence is heavier than other
people's words.

## Headline rules

- **8 words or less.** If a headline is longer, it's a paragraph.
- **Present tense, declarative.** No "Discover…", no "Imagine…", no
  rhetorical questions.
- **Subject + verb in the first three words.** "Sığortalı yola çıx" works.
  "Yeni bir növ səyahət sığortası ilə tanış olun" fails.
- **NO exclamation marks.** Ever. Even on celebratory copy.
- **NO ALL CAPS** except on a single accent word, never on the headline.
- One idea per headline. If two ideas, two headlines.

## Sub-headline rules

- **12 words or less.**
- Picks up the headline and gives the reason-to-care.
- Often the soft CTA lives here: "Sığortanı bu gün hazırla."

## Body copy rules

- **Per paragraph: 1–3 sentences.** Per sentence: aim for 15–20 words max
  in AZ, 12–18 in EN.
- **No padding adjectives.** "Müasir, sürətli, etibarlı" is amateur.
  Pick one concrete proof point instead.
- **No insurance jargon untranslated.** "Tarif," "limit," "ekvivalent
  zərər" — define or remove.
- **The legal microcopy is locked** (see `legal_phrases.md`). Never
  rewrite, never abbreviate, never translate.

## Caption rules (Instagram / LinkedIn)

- **First 2 lines carry everything** — they're what shows before "see more".
- **Hook → context → product → CTA.** In that order. No exceptions.
- Hashtags **3–5 strong**, never 15 generic. Brand + topic + region.
- **NO emoji on AZ primary captions.** EN cross-post may use sparingly
  (📞 for hotline, 🌐 for web) — never more than 2 per caption.

## Tonal range — when to flex

The voice has a centre and tolerable edges:

| Centre (default) | Flex toward (when?) |
|---|---|
| Calm authority | Warmer pace for family/holiday campaigns |
| Restrained | Slightly more urgent for deadline-driven (Jan 1 2026 type) |
| Plain declarative | One short rhetorical line allowed for heritage moments |

The centre is **never** flashy, never desperate, never youth-slang.
Even when targeting younger demographics, the voice stays adult — but
brevity and energy increase.

## Three-language posture

- **Azerbaijani (AZ):** primary. The voice's natural home.
- **English (EN):** for international LinkedIn / cross-post. Same calm
  authority. Avoid Americanisms ("crush it," "game-changer"). Reads more
  like Economist than TechCrunch.
- **Russian (RU):** as needed for the Russian-speaking AZ segment. Same
  voice, slight formality lift (vy-form throughout).

## When the LLM is writing in this voice

Hard reminders that come up repeatedly:

- ✗ "We are excited to announce…" → ALWAYS too marketing-y for this brand
- ✗ "Don't miss out!" → urgency-via-FOMO is not our register
- ✗ "Your future is in safe hands" → cliché financial-services
- ✗ "At Xalq Sigorta we believe…" → never lead with the brand
- ✗ Exclamation marks
- ✗ Emoji on AZ primary captions
- ✓ Lead with the customer's situation, not the brand's offer
- ✓ Use "siz" form in AZ (formal/respectful), "you" in EN
- ✓ When in doubt, cut a sentence. The voice's power is restraint.
