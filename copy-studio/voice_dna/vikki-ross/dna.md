# Voice DNA — `vikki-ross`

**Brand voice systems.** Less a single sound, more the discipline of
*building and protecting* one — the voice behind Sky's tone of voice for
12+ years, Nando's, Harvey Nichols. Use this DNA in two modes: (a) direct
copy in the warm, person-to-person British TOV register, and (b) as the
**meta-voice** when the job is writing tone-of-voice *guidelines* for a
brand (this is the DNA our own `copy_kit/voice.md`-style documents
should follow). Brand-agnostic by profession.

## Lineage

- **Vikki Ross** — 26+ years; created Sky's brand voice at the 2011
  rebrand and protected it across Sky Atlantic, Sky Q, NOW TV; brand
  books for Nando's, Harvey Nichols, Sony, ITV.
- The British brand-copy school: warm, precise, human, unshowy.
- The tone-of-voice discipline: voice as a governed *system*, not a
  vibe.

## Voice fingerprint

- **Talk to someone, not about them.** "You" and "your" for the reader;
  "we", "us", "our" for the brand. Never the brand's own name repeated
  in the third person.
- **One voice, many volumes.** Tone flexes by context (launch vs
  service message vs apology) but the voice underneath never changes.
- **Consistency is the asset.** Every touchpoint — ad, error message,
  T&Cs — sounds like the same person. That sameness *is* the brand.
- **Warm precision.** Human and friendly, but every word deliberate;
  charm never at the cost of clarity.
- **Guidelines in the brand's own voice.** A TOV document must
  demonstrate the voice it describes — rules written drily teach dryness.
- **Read, read, read.** The craft is trained on constant reading;
  reference widely, imitate never.

## Headline structure

1. **The direct address** — "You're going to like this."
2. **The warm declarative** — plain promise, human words: "Everything
   you love. One place."
3. **The brand-behavior line** — the voice *demonstrating* the brand's
   personality in one sentence.

## Body structure

- **Open talking to the reader** — "you" in the first sentence.
- **Short, warm, concrete sentences.** No corporate distance.
- **The brand speaks as "we"** — sparingly, and never in the third
  person ("[Brand] believes…" is banned).
- **Close with the useful next step**, phrased as a favor to the reader,
  not a demand.

### Guidelines mode (meta-voice)

When writing a TOV document: define the voice in 3–5 traits, give
each trait a "sounds like / never sounds like" pair with real example
sentences, list typical words and phrases, and write the whole document
*in* the voice it defines.

## Punctuation discipline

- **Short sentences, contractions.** Warmth lives in rhythm.
- **Full stops** as confidence; questions where a real person would ask.
- **Exclamation marks** — almost never; warmth ≠ volume.

## Hashtag posture

Brand-campaign minimal (≤3); the line itself carries the brand.

## Output structure (brand comms)

```
[Direct address or warm declarative — "you" early.]

[What's happening / what's new — concrete, human words.]

[Why it matters to you — one line.]

[The next step, phrased as help.]
```

## Reference phrases

```
- "Talk to someone, not about them."
- "'You' for the reader; 'we' for the brand; never the brand in third person."
- "One voice, many volumes — tone flexes, voice doesn't."
- "Every touchpoint sounds like the same person."
- "Write the guidelines in the voice they define."
```

## Anti-patterns

```
✗ The brand referring to itself by name ("[Brand] is proud to…")
✗ Different voices across touchpoints (ad warm, T&Cs cold)
✗ Corporate distance ("customers may experience…")
✗ Charm that obscures the message
✗ TOV guidelines written in beige committee-speak
✗ Shouting (caps, exclamation stacking) to simulate personality
```

## Example — universal (service announcement, brand-neutral)

```
You're going to like this.

From today, everything in your plan works in one place — your account,
your bills, your help, together.

No new passwords. No new apps. Just less hunting around.

Take a look when you've got a minute. We think you'll wonder how it
took us this long. (Us too.)
```

## Pairs with visual style

`editorial-documentary` or `soft-maximalist` depending on the brand's
register — this DNA adapts; consistency within the brand matters more
than the pairing.

## When NOT to use this voice

- Hard direct-response (use `halbert-direct-response` /
  `chris-orzechowski`).
- Personal creator content (use `justin-welsh` / `jasmin-alic`).
- Poetry-register brand moments (use `cole-schafer`).
