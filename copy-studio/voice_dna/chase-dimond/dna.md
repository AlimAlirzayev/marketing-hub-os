# Voice DNA — `chase-dimond`

Retention **email** copywriting. The voice for the flows that quietly
print money — welcome, abandoned cart, browse abandonment, post-purchase,
win-back — plus broadcast campaigns. Friendly, framework-built,
single-goal, mobile-first. Use this when the medium is the inbox and the
job is the next click. Brand-agnostic: the BRAND config supplies the
product and offer; the voice stays the same.

## Lineage

- **Chase Dimond** — email-led ecommerce growth ($200M+ attributed);
  "the money is in the flows."
- The Klaviyo / ecommerce-retention era: lifecycle email as a revenue
  system, not a newsletter.
- Direct-response heritage (Sugarman, Halbert) compressed for the inbox.

## Voice fingerprint

- **Never write from scratch.** Every email starts from a framework (PAS,
  AIDA, BAB — see `copy_kit/frameworks.md`), then gets the brand's voice.
- **One email, one job.** A single goal, a single primary CTA. If a
  second link exists, it's clearly secondary.
- **Conversational and human.** Writes like a helpful person, not a
  brand. Authentic and customer-centric beats polished and promotional.
- **Mobile-first.** Short lines, short paragraphs, scannable. Assume a
  thumb on a phone, three seconds of attention.
- **Subject + preview are one unit.** The subject opens a loop; the
  preview text extends it — never repeats it.
- **Segment-aware.** A first-time browser and a lapsed customer get
  different copy. The voice acknowledges where the reader is.

## Subject-line structure (the "headline" of email)

One of these, ≤ ~6 words, optimized for the mobile inbox:

1. **Curiosity loop** — "You left something behind…"
2. **Specific benefit** — "Your 15% is about to expire"
3. **Personal / pattern-interrupt** — "quick question"
4. **Urgency (honest)** — "last call: [thing] ends tonight"

Preview text closes or extends the loop — never duplicates the subject.

## Body structure

- **Open at the reason you're emailing** — no warm-up. The reader knows
  it's a brand; respect their time.
- **One idea, built on a framework.** PAS for problem flows, BAB for
  transformation, AIDA for launches.
- **Skimmable.** 1–2 sentence paragraphs. Bold or a button carries the
  CTA. White space is a feature.
- **One primary CTA, repeated** if the email is long (top + bottom).
- **P.S. earns its place** — restate the offer or add the single best
  reason to click.

Length: as short as the job allows. Flow emails are often 40–120 words.

## Punctuation discipline

- **Periods and short sentences.** Fragments fine for rhythm.
- **Em-dashes** for the aside.
- **Exclamation marks** — at most one, and only where genuine.
- **Emoji** — sparingly, brand-permitting; never as a crutch for a weak
  line.

## Hashtag posture

None — this is email, not social. Ignore the hashtag layer entirely.

## Output structure (flow email)

```
Subject: [curiosity / benefit loop — ≤6 words]
Preview: [extends the loop — never repeats the subject]

[Open at the reason — one line, no warm-up.]

[The single idea, built on a framework. 1–2 short paragraphs.]

[Primary CTA — one button, plain words: "Finish your order".]

P.S. [the single best reason to click now / the offer restated.]
```

## Anti-patterns

```
✗ Writing from a blank page instead of a proven framework
✗ Multiple competing CTAs in one email
✗ Long, desktop-era paragraphs that die on mobile
✗ Subject and preview text saying the same thing
✗ Generic promotional voice ("Dear valued customer…")
✗ Fake urgency / fake scarcity (real deadlines only)
✗ Burying the reason for the email below a warm-up paragraph
```

## Example — universal (abandoned-cart email, brand-neutral)

```
Subject: You left something behind…
Preview: It's still in your cart (for now).

Hey — you were *this* close.

The [product] is still in your cart, but we can only hold it for a
little while. Sizes/colors go fast.

Want us to finish checkout for you?

→ Return to your cart

P.S. Questions before you buy? Just reply — a real person reads these.
```

The difference from `halbert-direct-response` is register: Halbert is the
hard deadline press; Dimond is the friendly nudge from someone who
remembers you. Same direct-response bones, inbox manners.

## Pairs with visual style

`editorial-documentary` for lifestyle ecommerce, or `soft-maximalist` for
playful DTC brands. Keep imagery light — email renders on a phone.

## When NOT to use this voice

- Landing / sales pages (use `joanna-wiebe`).
- Brand-prestige moments (use `apple-jobsian` / `hermes-quiet-luxury`).
- Long-form thought-leadership (use `justin-welsh`).
