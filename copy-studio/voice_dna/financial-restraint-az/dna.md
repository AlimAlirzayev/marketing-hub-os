# Voice DNA — `financial-restraint-az`

The Xalq Sigorta default voice for AZ-primary copy. Sakit professional
Azerbaijani. The voice of the senior at the table whose silence is
heavier than other people's words. **This is the centre of the brand
voice** — every other DNA flexes off this one.

## Lineage

- **Pasha Bank** corporate communications (the serious-tier ones, not
  the retail-cheery ones).
- **Kapital Bank** annual report and investor-day language.
- **Xalq Sigorta historical print campaigns** (the heritage materials
  in `brand_kit/` reference).
- **Allianz Azerbaijan** Russian-language financial-services copy.
- **The Economist** AZ-translated quarterly briefings (where they exist).

## Voice fingerprint

- **Sakit, dəqiq, az emosional.** Calm, precise, low-emotional.
- **"Siz" form throughout.** Formal address, respect-default.
- **No hype.** No "möhtəşəm", no "fenomenal", no "inanılmaz".
- **Specifics over abstractions.** Numbers, dates, addresses appear in
  body without translation.
- **The brand earns the customer's time** by being brief and useful,
  not by being loud.
- **Insurance-industry-specific.** This voice knows the customer is
  making a financial decision and treats them accordingly.

## Headline structure

The financial-restraint-az headline is one of these:

1. **The Plain Statement**
   - "Səyahət sığortası məcburidir."
   - "Sığorta üç dəqiqəyə hazırdır."
   - Format: [topic] + [verb] + [period]

2. **The Reason-To-Act (no urgency yelling)**
   - "Bakı–Tbilisi yolu açıldı. Sənədin də hazırdır."
   - Format: [event observed] + [implication observed]

3. **The Service Promise (restrained)**
   - "Onlayn sığorta. Bir telefonda."
   - "Hotline 183. Filiallarda da."

Length: 4-8 words. Period always. Never exclamation.

## Sub-headline structure

The sub adds the single specific that makes the headline land — a date,
a duration, a price, a phone number.

- "1 yanvar 2026-dan etibarən."
- "Üç dəqiqədə bir sənəddə."
- "183 — bir zəng."

## Body copy structure

- **Open with a fact, not the brand.** "Bakı–Tbilisi qatarı saat 21:40-da
  yola düşür." — not "Bizim sığortamız…"
- **Each paragraph one specific.** A number, a date, an address, a
  duration, a comparison.
- **Soft CTA as the last sentence.** Phone or URL on its own line.
- **No exclamation marks.** No emoji. The body's calm is the voice's
  authority.

Average length: 60-110 words. Less is more.

## Punctuation discipline

- **Period** as primary. **Comma** for parsing lists.
- **Em-dash** sparingly — for the side-fact.
- **Colon** allowed for a sub-list.
- **Semicolon** allowed in formal-register body.
- **NO** exclamation marks. **NO** rhetorical question marks.
- **Ellipses** never (signal hesitation; brand never hesitates).

## Hashtag posture

- **2-4 hashtags maximum.** Brand + product + region.
- PascalCase (#XalqSigorta), separated by middle-dot for visual lift.
- Format: `#XalqSigorta · #SəyahətSığortası · #BakıTbilisi`
- No emoji-hashtags, no joke-hashtags.

## Caption structure

```
[Plain-statement headline — 4-8 words, period.]

[Sub — single specific that makes it land.]

[Body paragraph 1 — the customer's situation today. ONE fact.]

[Body paragraph 2 — the product / path. ONE fact.]

[Mandatory legal phrase — verbatim per legal_phrases.md.]

[Soft CTA — phone, URL, branches. Each on its own line if space.]

#XalqSigorta · #ProductTag · #RegionTag
```

## Anti-patterns

```
✗ "Möhtəşəm", "fenomenal", "inanılmaz" — over-used hype adjectives
✗ "Sevimli müştərilərimiz" — paternalistic
✗ "Bilirikkı...", "Biz hesab edirikkı..." — corporate filler
✗ "Hörmətli müştərilər" — old-fashioned formal opener
✗ "Səbrsizliklə gözləyirik" — desperate
✗ "Heç bir problem olmadan" — double-negative
✗ Exclamation marks
✗ Emoji on AZ primary captions
✗ "Bizim üçün ən vacibi..." — never lead with the brand
✗ Vague benefit claims without a specific
```

## Example — Xalq Sigorta travel insurance, AZ voice

### Headline options

```
1. Bakı–Tbilisi yolu açıldı. Sığortanız da hazırdır.
2. Səyahət sığortası 1 yanvar 2026-dan məcburidir.
3. Onlayn üç dəqiqə. Filiallarda beş.
```

### Caption (full financial-restraint-az structure)

```
Bakı–Tbilisi yolu açıldı. Sığortanız da hazırdır.

1 yanvar 2026-dan etibarən Gürcüstana giriş üçün
səyahət sığortası məcburidir.

Bakı–Tbilisi qatarı saat 21:40-da yola düşür. Səyahət
on iki saat çəkir. Sığortanın hazırlanması — üç dəqiqə.

Onlayn: xalqsigorta.az
Hotline: 183
Filiallar: günün hər saatı.

#XalqSigorta · #SəyahətSığortası · #BakıTbilisi
```

The difference from the international voices (Ogilvy, Apple, Hermès,
Halbert) is the **specifically-Azerbaijani-corporate** rhythm:

- "Siz" form is built into every clause.
- Time + duration + channel pattern in body ("21:40-da yola düşür / 12
  saat çəkir / 3 dəqiqə hazır").
- Soft CTA channel-list rather than urgency.
- Sentences slightly more formal than the EN equivalent.

This is the default for Xalq Sigorta AZ copy. Use it unless the
campaign-specific brief explicitly calls for a different voice.

## When this voice IS the right choice

- Default for all Xalq Sigorta AZ-primary captions
- Product comms (insurance category communications)
- Regulatory-driven announcements
- B2B / partnership communications
- Anything where the customer needs information to act

## When to use a different voice instead

- Brand-prestige moments → `apple-jobsian`
- Hard-deadline urgency campaigns → `halbert-direct-response`
- Heritage / craft positioning → `hermes-quiet-luxury`
- International product-comparison campaigns → `ogilvy-product`
