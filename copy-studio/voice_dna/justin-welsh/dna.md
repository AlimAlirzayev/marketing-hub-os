# Voice DNA — `justin-welsh`

**Personal-brand / thought-leadership** copy for the feed — LinkedIn and
X posts, newsletter essays, founder and executive content. The voice that
turns one person's hard-won lessons into a following. A different job from
the rest of the library: not selling a product, but building authority
and trust at scale. Brand-agnostic — works for a solopreneur, a founder,
or an executive speaking for a company.

## Lineage

- **Justin Welsh** — solopreneur; *The Saturday Solopreneur*; 500M+
  yearly content impressions from a repeatable system.
- The LinkedIn/X organic-creator tradition: hook → value → light CTA.
- Direct-response hook discipline applied to social feeds.

## Voice fingerprint

- **The hook line is everything.** On the feed, the first line decides
  whether the rest is read (the "…more" click). It must intrigue.
- **Write the body first, then the hook.** Get the idea out, *then*
  craft the line that pulls people into it.
- **One lesson per post.** A single, transferable insight — earned from
  real experience, not theory.
- **Pain-first (PAIPS).** Open by naming a pain the reader feels, then
  move to the insight and the practical takeaway.
- **Plain, confident, generous.** Short sentences. No jargon. Give the
  actual lesson away — the authority comes from usefulness.
- **Systematized, not precious.** One idea becomes a newsletter, then
  6–12 short posts. Built to repurpose.
- **Scannable.** Line breaks, lists, white space — built for the phone.

## Hook structure (the first line is the headline)

1. **The pain hook** — name the exact frustration: "Most people will
   spend 40 years building someone else's dream."
2. **The contrarian take** — "Hustle culture is lying to you."
3. **The result + timeframe** — "I built a $5M business with zero
   employees. Here's the system."
4. **The numbered promise** — "7 lessons from 5 years of writing online."

The hook opens a loop; the body closes it. Never waste the first line on
a warm-up.

## Body structure

- **Hook** — one line, intriguing, before the "…more" fold.
- **Context** — 1–2 lines that set up the lesson (the pain, amplified).
- **The lesson** — the insight, broken into short lines or a tight list.
- **Make it practical** — what the reader can do with it today.
- **Soft close** — a one-line takeaway or a light question; a hard sell
  here breaks trust. (A CTA, if any, is gentle: follow / subscribe.)

Length: feed posts 100–250 words; newsletter essays longer but same
bones.

## Punctuation discipline

- **Short sentences. Periods.** The default register.
- **Line breaks** carry the rhythm — often one line per paragraph.
- **Colons and dashes** to set up lists and reveals.
- **Exclamation marks** — rare; confidence doesn't shout.
- **Lists** (numbered / bulleted) for scannability.

## Hashtag posture

Minimal (0–3). On LinkedIn the body carries reach, not tags. Topic-first
if used at all.

## Output structure (LinkedIn / newsletter post)

```
[Hook — one intriguing line before the fold.]

[Context — the pain, named. 1–2 lines.]

[The lesson:]
- [point]
- [point]
- [point]

[Make it practical — what to do today.]

[Soft close — one-line takeaway or light question.]
```

## Anti-patterns

```
✗ A weak first line that buries the hook below the "…more" fold
✗ Multiple lessons crammed into one post
✗ Theory with no lived, specific experience behind it
✗ Hard-selling in a trust-building post
✗ Jargon and corporate hedging ("leverage synergies")
✗ Walls of text with no line breaks
✗ Engagement-bait that doesn't deliver real value
```

## Example — universal (LinkedIn post, brand-neutral)

```
I wasted my first 3 years writing online.

Not because I wasn't consistent. I posted every week.

The problem? I wrote what I thought sounded smart.
Nobody cared.

The day it changed, I started writing one thing:

The lesson I wish someone had told me a year earlier.

That's it. That's the whole strategy.

- Pick one hard thing you've actually figured out.
- Write the lesson, not the theory.
- Make the first line impossible to scroll past.

Do that weekly for a year and you won't recognize your audience.

What's one lesson you learned the hard way?
```

The difference from the conversion voices is intent: `joanna-wiebe` moves
a stranger to buy; `justin-welsh` earns a stranger's trust over months so
that, eventually, the sell is easy. This is the brand-building voice —
the long game.

## Pairs with visual style

`editorial-documentary` or `financial-restraint` — credible, plain,
founder-authentic. Avoid heavy art direction; this voice reads as a
person, not a campaign.

## When NOT to use this voice

- Direct selling / conversion pages (use `joanna-wiebe` /
  `halbert-direct-response`).
- Email flows (use `chase-dimond`).
- Brand-prestige poetry (use `cole-schafer`).
