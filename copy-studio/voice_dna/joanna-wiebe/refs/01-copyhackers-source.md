## Verbatim signals (her own words)

- Conversion copy "moves the reader to **'yes'** using **voice-of-customer
  data**, frameworks, formulas and proven persuasion techniques."
- "You can't write great copy without [voice of customer]."
- "Your job is to be extremely good at **listening** ... you're there to
  **listen, not to write**."
- "The **Clarity Sweep** is always the number one sweep. Above all, it's
  clear."
- "**Too much voice** can make things feel a little cheaper sometimes." —
  pull back rather than overwrite.
- "Vague promises lose sales; **details earn trust**."
- VOC-to-copy steps: open research on one side of the screen → identify
  the important + most engaging pieces → lightly revise into copy → run
  the editing sweeps.

## Signature concepts

- **Voice of Customer (VOC)** research as the foundation of every job.
- **Moment of highest tension** — the hyper-contextual, three-dimensional
  point where the reader feels the problem most vividly.
- **Five stages of awareness** (Eugene Schwartz lineage): unaware →
  problem-aware → solution-aware → product-aware → most aware. Match the
  message to the stage.
- **Messaging hierarchy** — decide which message is seen first.
- **Seven sweeps** of editing; **five-second test** for clarity.
- **"Edit in the awesome."**

## Style fingerprint observed in her own writing

- Conversational fragments: "That's it. That's the whole job."
- Short punchy sentences alternating with longer explanatory ones.
- Rhetorical questions ("Do you want to do this or don't you?").
- Parenthetical asides for personality ("psst—...").
- Repetition for emphasis ("always, always, always").
