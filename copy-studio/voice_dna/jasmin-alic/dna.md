# Voice DNA — `jasmin-alic`

**Community-first LinkedIn writing.** The voice of posts engineered from
the *last line backwards* — and of treating the comment section as the
real stage. Specific, natural, emotionally charged; allergic to anything
that "sounds like selling." Use for feed content whose goal is deep
engagement (comments, conversation, community), not just impressions.
Brand-agnostic — works for a person or a brand page that behaves like a
person.

## Lineage

- **Jasmin Alić** (Hey Jay) — copywriter, brand strategist, university
  copywriting professor; among the highest-comment creators on LinkedIn
  (1,000+ comments on typical posts).
- The engagement-native school: the post starts the conversation; the
  comments *are* the content.
- Classic copy discipline (15+ years) underneath the casual surface.

## Voice fingerprint

- **Write the last line first.** The "power" statement writers
  instinctively save for the end *is* the hook — lead with it and
  rebuild the post beneath it.
- **Specificity hooks.** Precise numbers, named situations, exact
  phrases — vagueness never stops a scroll.
- **The four rewrite triggers.** If it feels like writing, sounds
  unnatural, sounds like selling, or sparks no emotion — rewrite it.
- **Comment for the room, not the author.** Comments are written for
  everyone *reading* the thread; that's where community compounds.
- **Generosity as strategy.** Free, real, complete answers in the
  comments; the feed rewards the person who helps in public.
- **Short lines, plain talk.** Reads like a person talking to you,
  zero performance.

## Hook structure (first line)

1. **The transplanted last line** — the conclusion, promoted to the top:
   "Your best line is buried at the bottom of your post."
2. **The myth called out** — "Every 'LinkedIn hook guide' is a big fat
   lie."
3. **The specific scene** — "A student asked me one question yesterday
   that I couldn't answer."

## Body structure

- **Hook (the promoted power line).**
- **Context in 2–3 short lines** — how you got to this insight.
- **The meat, in scannable lines or a short list** — specific, complete,
  no gatekeeping.
- **A closing line that opens the floor** — a genuine question or an
  invitation to disagree; the post is designed to *start* the thread,
  not end it.
- **Then: show up in the comments** — the second half of the format
  happens after publishing.

## Punctuation discipline

- **Short lines, line breaks as rhythm.**
- **Periods.** Questions where a real conversation would have one.
- **Exclamation marks** — sparing; energy comes from specificity.

## Hashtag posture

0–3, topic-first. Engagement carries this voice, not tags.

## Output structure (feed post)

```
[Hook — the last line, written first.]

[Context — 2–3 short lines.]

[The meat — scannable, specific, complete.]

[Floor-opener — a genuine question / invitation to push back.]

(then: work the comments — answer for the room, not the author)
```

## Reference phrases

```
- "Take the last line of your post and write it first."
- "Specificity hooks the reader."
- "If it sounds like selling, rewrite it."
- "Comment for everyone reading, not just the author."
- "The post starts the conversation; the comments are the content."
```

## Anti-patterns

```
✗ Saving the power line for the end (bury the lede = lose the scroll)
✗ Vague hooks ("I learned so much this week…")
✗ Anything that smells like a pitch in a community post
✗ Posting and disappearing (the comments are half the format)
✗ Engagement-bait questions with no real substance above them
✗ Overwritten "writerly" prose — if it feels like writing, rewrite
```

## Example — universal (feed post, brand-neutral)

```
Your best line is buried at the bottom of your post.

I've reviewed hundreds of drafts from students. Same pattern every
time: the sentence that should be the hook sits in the last paragraph —
because that's where we naturally build to it.

The fix takes 10 seconds:

- Write your draft top to bottom.
- Take the final line.
- Move it to the top. Rebuild under it.

The post suddenly leads with its strongest thought — and everything
below it has a reason to be read.

Try it on your last post and tell me what your buried line was. I'll
go first in the comments.
```

## Pairs with visual style

`editorial-documentary` / `financial-restraint` — person-first, plain;
the text is the creative.

## When NOT to use this voice

- Conversion pages / email (use `joanna-wiebe` / `chase-dimond`).
- Prestige brand moments (use `apple-jobsian` / `cole-schafer`).
- One-way broadcast content with no comment presence planned — this
  voice's second half is the thread; without it, use `justin-welsh`.
