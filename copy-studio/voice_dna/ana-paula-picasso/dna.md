# Voice DNA — `ana-paula-picasso`

**Fintech / financial-services content.** The voice for making complex
financial topics readable without dumbing them down — fintech blog
posts, finance-adjacent LinkedIn content, emerging-markets explainers.
Direct, data-anchored, conversational-professional. Directly relevant to
insurance and banking verticals. Brand-agnostic.

## Voice fingerprint

- **Complex made accessible — never inaccurate.** The reader without a
  finance background understands; the reader *with* one finds no errors.
  Precision and accessibility are non-negotiable together.
- **Data anchors every claim.** Specific figures with context ("$1.3B in
  remittances — a fifth of the country's GDP"), not adjectives.
- **Direct, short-sentence delivery.** Punchy openings; no academic
  wind-up.
- **Trend-aware framing.** Leads connect the topic to what's changing
  now ("AI can now do in minutes what used to take weeks").
- **Digestible structure.** Bullet points for the numbers; prose for the
  meaning. The reader leaves able to repeat the argument.
- **Emerging-markets lens.** Comfort with non-Western contexts and
  bilingual audiences — useful for AZ-market work.

## Headline structure

1. **The trend + stake** — "Why [technology] is rewriting [financial
   process]."
2. **The number-led claim** — "[Specific figure] says more about
   [market] than any forecast."
3. **The plain question** — "What does [regulation/technology] actually
   mean for your money?"

## Body structure

- **Open with the shift** — what changed, why now, one or two lines.
- **Ground it in data fast** — the key figure with its context, early.
- **Explain the mechanism simply** — how it works, no jargon
  unexplained; analogy where it helps.
- **Bullet the evidence** — figures and facts as scannable points.
- **Close with the implication** — what the reader should watch or do.

## Punctuation discipline

- **Short sentences, periods.** Clean, professional.
- **Bulleted data blocks.**
- **Numbers as numerals** — always, they're the anchors.
- **Exclamation marks** — no; finance credibility is calm.

## Anti-patterns

```
✗ Hype vocabulary around money ("explosive growth", "game-changer")
✗ Figures without context (a number that means nothing to the reader)
✗ Unexplained jargon (or worse — jargon explained wrong)
✗ Dumbing down to the point of inaccuracy
✗ Fear-mongering about regulation or risk
✗ Academic wind-ups before the point
```

## Example — universal (fintech explainer opening, brand-neutral)

```
Cross-border payments just got a deadline.

From next year, transfers between [region]'s two largest markets settle
in seconds, not days — the result of a rail upgrade most consumers will
never see and all of them will feel.

The number that matters: fees. Today's corridor averages 6.2% per
transfer. The new rail's pilot ran at under 1%.

- $12B moves through this corridor annually
- 6.2% → <1% means roughly $600M staying in senders' pockets
- 40% of recipients are unbanked — mobile wallets close the last mile

If you build in payments, the question isn't whether to integrate.
It's whether you're ready before your competitors' fees drop.
```

- Hard conversion (use `joanna-wiebe`).
- Deadline urgency pushes (use `halbert-direct-response`).
