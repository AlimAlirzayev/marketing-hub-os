# Voice DNA — `halbert-direct-response`

Time-bound, conversion-driven, specific. For deadline campaigns
(the Jan 1 2026 mandatory insurance situation is the perfect fit),
limited offers, anything where the user must act this week not next month.

## Lineage

- **Gary Halbert**, "The Halbert Letter" — the modern direct-response
  godfather.
- **John Caples**, "Tested Advertising Methods" — the foundational
  long-copy direct-response treatise.
- **Joanna Wiebe**, Copyhackers (the conversion-optimization era).
- **Eugene Schwartz**, "Breakthrough Advertising" — for the underlying
  awareness-state framework.

## Voice fingerprint

- **Specific numbers everywhere.** "31 days left." "Only 4 hours." "3
  minutes online." Vague is the enemy.
- **One CTA per piece.** Strong, hard, unmissable. Same CTA
  reinforced 2-3 times if length permits.
- **Curiosity-driving opener.** The first sentence makes the reader want
  to know the next.
- **Urgency without manipulation.** Real deadlines, not fake scarcity.
  The Jan 1 2026 date is real; we can lean on it hard.
- **Direct address.** "Sən" / "you" — never the corporate "siz"
  except where AZ formality requires.

Halbert voice ≠ spammy. It is **honest urgency about a real time-bound
event**. When there's no real deadline, this voice loses.

## Headline structure

Halbert headlines are one of these:

1. **The Specific Deadline**
   - "31 days until Gürcüstana giriş tələbi dəyişir."
   - "1 yanvar 2026-a 7 ay qalıb."

2. **The Number-Led Promise**
   - "3 dəqiqə. Sığorta hazırdır."
   - "183. Tək rəqəm. Tək həll."

3. **The Curiosity Gap**
   - "Niyə bütün uzaq səfərlər indi sığortalıdır?"
   - "Bakı–Tbilisi qatarında ən önəmli sənəd."

Length: 6-10 words. Rhetorical questions allowed (rare for our other
voices, mandatory for some Halbert headlines).

## Sub-headline structure

The sub closes the curiosity gap from the headline or restates the
urgency.

- "1 yanvar 2026-dan giriş üçün məcburidir."
- "Onlayn üç dəqiqə. Filialda beş."

## Body copy structure

- **Open with the specific problem the reader has TODAY.**
- **Build the case with 2-3 specific facts (numbers, dates, names).**
- **Close with the action — single CTA, often repeated.**
- **Soft P.S. at the end** — Halbert's signature move, where you put
  the second strongest CTA.

Average length: 80-140 words for Instagram caption, 150-220 for LinkedIn.

## Punctuation discipline

- **Periods** as primary.
- **Question marks** in headlines and sometimes in sub.
- **Em-dashes** for emphasis — used.
- **Exclamation marks** — STILL no. Even Halbert at his most urgent
  uses periods. The urgency comes from the words, not the punctuation.
- **Ellipses** acceptable in body for narrative pauses ("And then…").

## Hashtag posture

- 4-5 hashtags maximum. Brand + product + deadline + region.
- Format: PascalCase.
- One hashtag may carry the deadline (#YanvarDeadline, #2026Deadline).

## Caption structure (Instagram / LinkedIn)

```
[Curiosity / deadline headline — 6-10 words.]

[Sub — closes the gap, names the specific.]

[Body paragraph 1 — the reader's situation. ONE specific fact.]

[Body paragraph 2 — the product/path. ONE specific fact.]

[Body paragraph 3 — the action. CTA + specific channel.]

[P.S. — restate the deadline.]

#XalqSigorta · #ProductTag · #DeadlineTag
```

## Reference phrases

```
- "Direct-response copywriting tradition — Gary Halbert, John Caples,
   Joanna Wiebe."
- "Specific numbers everywhere. Vague is the enemy."
- "One CTA per piece, repeated 2-3 times if length permits."
- "Honest urgency about a real time-bound event."
- "P.S. carries the second-strongest CTA — signature move."
```

## Anti-patterns

```
✗ "Limited time only!!!" — fake scarcity
✗ "Act NOW!" — caps-shouty
✗ Multiple CTAs (one channel, two channels max, with one primary)
✗ "Don't miss out" — passive-aggressive
✗ Vague benefit claims ("amazing", "incredible")
✗ Exclamation marks despite the urgency (still no)
✗ Adjective stacking
✗ Hyperbolic numbers ("100% guaranteed", "infinite")
```

## Example — Xalq Sigorta travel insurance, Halbert voice

### Headline options

```
1. 1 yanvar 2026-a 7 ay qalıb.
2. Bakı–Tbilisi qatarı yola düşür. Sənədin hazırdır?
3. 3 dəqiqə onlayn. Sığorta cibində.
```

### Caption (full Halbert structure, AZ)

```
1 yanvar 2026-a 7 ay qalıb.

Gürcüstana giriş üçün səyahət sığortası məcburidir.

Bakı–Tbilisi qatarı saat 21:40-da yola düşür.
Səhər saat 9:30-da artıq Tbilisidəsən.

Sığorta üç dəqiqəni alır. Onlayn xalqsigorta.az-da
ya da 183 nömrəsi ilə bir zəng.

Hazırlamadığın halda — 1 yanvar səhəri sərhəddə
geri qayıdırsan.

P.S. Sığorta indidən səyahət gününə qədər keçərlidir.
Bir dəfə hazırla, ilboyu rahat ol.

#XalqSigorta · #BakıTbilisi · #SəyahətSığortası · #2026Deadline
```

The difference from Ogilvy is the **urgency anchor**: Halbert leads with
the deadline number, builds the case with specific facts (train time,
how many minutes the sign-up takes), and closes with the consequence of
inaction ("sərhəddə geri qayıdırsan") + the P.S. ("ilboyu rahat ol").

Ogilvy gives evidence. Halbert gives evidence + the cost of not acting.

## When NOT to use this voice

- Heritage / brand-prestige moments (use `apple-jobsian` or `hermes-quiet-luxury`)
- Anything without a real deadline (the voice fails without genuine urgency)
- Long-funnel awareness campaigns (use `ogilvy-product`)
- Premium-tier B2B (use `financial-restraint-az`)
