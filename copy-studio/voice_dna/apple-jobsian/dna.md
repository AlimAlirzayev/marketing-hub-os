# Voice DNA — `apple-jobsian`

Restraint as luxury. The headline that says everything by saying almost
nothing. For Xalq Sigorta brand-prestige moments — anniversaries, major
product launches, the rare "we are the brand" statement.

## Lineage

- **Apple under Steve Jobs** (1997-2011) and the TBWA\Media Arts Lab
  copy tradition (Lee Clow, Ken Segall, James Vincent).
- **Nike's "Just Do It"** — Wieden+Kennedy original — same minimalism
  with more attitude.
- **Patek Philippe's "You never actually own a Patek Philippe"** — the
  long-tail equivalent of jobsian restraint.
- **Tesla early product copy** — before they lost the plot.

## Voice fingerprint

- **Headline ≤4 words.** "Think different." "1000 songs in your pocket."
  "The new iPhone." Anything longer is verbose.
- **Period as the only punctuation.** No exclamation, no comma, rarely a
  colon.
- **One product truth per piece.** If you find yourself listing features,
  you're not in this voice.
- **Negative space in language.** Where Ogilvy explains, jobsian implies.
- **Confidence without argument.** The product earned the right to be
  brief; we are just stating what it is.

## Headline structure

The jobsian headline is one of these:

1. **The Two-Word Imperative**
   - "Think different."
   - "Get ready."
   - "Yola çıx." (Set off.)

2. **The Three-Word Statement**
   - "1000 songs in your pocket."
   - "The new iPhone."
   - "Yeni reys. Sakit səyahət." (debatable as 4 words depending on count)

3. **The Single Object**
   - "iPhone."
   - "The Touch Bar."
   - "Sığorta. Hazırdır."

Length: 1-4 words. Period mandatory. NEVER an exclamation mark.

## Sub-headline structure

The sub is the one sentence that makes the headline land.

- Always ONE sentence. Always ≤10 words.
- States the single truth the headline implies.

Examples:
- Headline: "Think different." → Sub: "For the crazy ones."
- Headline: "1000 songs in your pocket." → (no sub; the headline carries everything)
- Headline: "Yola çıx." → Sub: "Sığortayı biz hazırlayırıq."

## Body copy structure

This is the voice's hardest discipline. Body is:

- **1-2 short paragraphs maximum.**
- **One sentence per idea.** Sometimes one word per sentence.
- **No marketing connectives.** No "moreover," no "additionally," no
  "as you can see." The reader gets it.
- **Period as breath mark.** Sentences end where breath ends.

Example (Apple iPad launch):

```
A magical and revolutionary device at an unbelievable price.
What is iPad? iPad is our most advanced technology in a magical
and revolutionary device.

It's the best way to experience the web, email, photos, and video.
```

(Note: this is the actual Apple iPad copy. Today's jobsian voice has
slightly evolved past "magical and revolutionary" — the principle of
restraint is preserved, the hype words have aged out.)

## Punctuation discipline

- **Period.** Always.
- **Comma.** Sparingly. Pairs of items only.
- **Colon.** Once per piece maximum, if at all.
- **Em-dash.** Rarely. Only when the rhythm demands the pause.
- **NO** semicolons, ellipses, exclamation marks, question marks in headlines.

## Hashtag posture

- **0-2 hashtags maximum.** Often zero — the voice is too confident for
  hashtag clutter.
- If used: brand only. #XalqSigorta. That's it. No topic, no region.

## Caption structure (rare — this voice favors visual over copy)

Standard jobsian caption is:

```
[Headline — 1-4 words, period.]

[Sub — one sentence, ≤10 words.]

[Body — 1-2 short paragraphs at most. May be omitted entirely.]

[CTA — minimal: just the URL. No phone, no branches.]
```

Many jobsian posts have NO caption — the visual is the post, the
caption is just the brand handle.

## Reference phrases (for prompt instructions)

```
- "Apple-Jobsian voice: headline ≤4 words, period as punctuation,
   confidence without argument."
- "One product truth per piece. If listing features, wrong voice."
- "Negative space in language — what is implied weighs more than
   what is said."
- "Body 1-2 short paragraphs at most. Often no body."
- "Hashtags 0-2 max. Often zero."
```

## Anti-patterns

```
✗ Headlines over 4 words
✗ Adjective stacking
✗ "Discover", "Imagine", "Experience" rhetorical openers
✗ Exclamation marks
✗ Multiple hashtags
✗ Feature lists
✗ "We are excited / proud / thrilled" anywhere
✗ Question marks in headlines
✗ Emoji
✗ Long body copy (>50 words)
```

## Example — Xalq Sigorta travel insurance, jobsian voice

### Headline options

```
1. Yola çıx.
2. Yeni reys.
3. Sığorta. Hazır.
4. Sakit səyahət.
```

### Caption (full jobsian structure, AZ)

```
Yola çıx.

Sığortanı biz hazırlayırıq.

xalqsigorta.az

#XalqSigorta
```

That's the full Instagram caption. Three lines of text plus brand hashtag.

### EN cross-post

```
Set off.

We handle the rest.

xalqsigorta.az

#XalqSigorta
```

The difference from Ogilvy is total: Ogilvy gives evidence, jobsian
gives the conclusion. Use this voice when the product / brand has
earned the right to skip the argument — anniversaries, major launches,
prestige moments. Not for everyday product comms.

## When NOT to use this voice

- Topical / news-tied campaigns (the voice is too restrained for
  "the train just opened" energy — use `ogilvy-product` or
  `halbert-direct-response`)
- Regulatory-driven announcements (mandatory insurance, etc. — needs
  evidence + reason-to-act, not just a 4-word command)
- Family / lifestyle products (too cool, too distant)
- B2B / partnership communications (use `financial-restraint-az` instead)
