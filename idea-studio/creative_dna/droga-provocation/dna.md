# Creative DNA — `droga-provocation`

**Earned media by design + brand acts.** The Droga5 tradition: the idea
itself is the media plan — engineered to be talked about, provocation
anchored to a defensible purpose, brands that *act* rather than merely
advertise. Canonical proofs: UNICEF Tap Project, Marc Ecko "Still Free",
Under Armour "I Will What I Want", NYT "The Truth Is Hard". Use when the
budget is small and the ambition is news.

## Lineage

- **David Droga**, Droga5 (2006–) — "you have to earn your way into
  their attention."
- The brand-act school: the mechanic delivers the purpose, copy just
  explains it.

## Idea fingerprint

- **Creativity is leverage, not decoration.** Start from the client's
  commercial/societal problem; let the idea solve it. The work is judged
  by the problem it moved.
- **Earn attention instead of buying it.** Build in the reason
  journalists must cover it and people must retell it; paid media is
  ignition, never the engine.
- **Brand acts, not brand ads.** Invent something the brand *does* or
  the audience *can do* that IS the belief in action — "we are not just
  creating advertising that fits into a 30-second commercial."
- **Provoke with a defensible purpose.** The shock must literalize a
  real argument, so the reveal converts outrage into thesis instead of
  apology ("Still Free" = protest against anti-graffiti laws).
- **The real world is the jury.** "You're good if your peers think
  you're great, but you're great if the real world thinks you're good."
- **Idea before execution.** Lock the provocative core first; then
  choose the medium it demands.
- **Non-disposable platforms.** Repeatable mechanics (Tap Project ran a
  decade), extendable manifestos ("Truth Is Hard" → "Truth Is Worth It").
- **"More lighthouse work, not just fireworks."** A stunt must set
  long-term direction, not just flash.
- **Restraint as craft.** The strongest provocations are starkly simple
  (black text on white; one unpolished take) — the idea, not the gloss,
  carries the charge.
- **Stay culturally live.** Anchor to what people argue about *now*;
  relevance makes coverage inevitable.

## Devices (operational)

| Device | Instruction | Canonical proof |
|---|---|---|
| **Stunt-with-meaning** | One audacious, physically REAL act that appears to violate a sacred rule; shot raw like leaked footage; forensic believability; the act literalizes the thesis so debunking = amplification. Budget the reveal. | "Still Free" (2006) — the Pentagon had to deny it |
| **Manifesto film** | One repeated syntactic frame ("The truth is…") escalating through a live cultural conflict; stark unadorned form; premiere inside one high-attention broadcast moment. | NYT, Oscars 2017 — more subs in 24h than prior 6 weeks |
| **Product-as-proof (brand act)** | Brand a free/overlooked thing + attach a simple transaction with an explicit exchange rate ($1 = 40 days of clean water). Mechanic must be repeatable and scale without the agency. | UNICEF Tap Project (2007) |
| **Engineered backlash harvest** | Trigger a predictable public argument (controversial casting announced early, unexplained); build the hero execution around the REAL hostile comments, displayed verbatim, visibly overcome. Never fabricate. | Under Armour — Gisele (2015) |
| **Real artifact as script** | Voiceover from a genuine document of rejection/doubt, read flat, while visuals refute it physically. | Misty Copeland's real rejection letter (2014); +28% women's sales |
| **Unbranded seeding + authored reveal** | Seed anonymous through non-ad channels → let media investigate → reveal at coverage peak with the thesis statement. | "Still Free" rollout |

## Anti-patterns

```
✗ Shock without thesis (fireworks, no lighthouse → apology at unmasking)
✗ Buying attention: if the plan collapses without paid budget, there's no engine
✗ Disposable one-offs with no second chapter designed in
✗ Peer-award creativity (juries over civilians)
✗ Fake authenticity — a fabricated artifact turns the reveal into a scandal
✗ Celebrity as substitute for idea (the casting must CREATE the tension)
✗ House-style templating ("doing the same thing over and over")
✗ Purpose-washing — purpose must be delivered by the mechanic, not claimed in copy
```

## When to use

- Small budget, big ambition; cause-adjacent briefs; moments when the
  category is culturally live.
- Insurance fit: **brand acts** — don't say "biz qayğı göstəririk",
  build the mechanic that proves it (e.g. a real free-inspection day, a
  storm-warning service) and let the act be the campaign.

## When NOT to use

- The brand can't defend the provocation when unmasked (no real thesis).
- Risk-intolerant regulated moments — the backlash-harvest device
  needs a client who holds nerve.
- Long-funnel product-truth jobs (use `bernbach-ddb`).
