# Creative DNA — `wieden-kennedy`

**The emotional anthem + cultural courage.** Advertising built on a lived
human truth rather than a product feature: the belief stated over an
escalating montage, landing on a 3–4 word imperative ("Just Do It") — or
its comic twin, the one-take absurdist monologue (Old Spice). The brand
takes a real position and lets the product ride along instead of leading.
Use when the job is to make people *feel* who the brand is.

## Lineage

- **Dan Wieden & David Kennedy**, W+K Portland — Nike from 1982; "we
  kind of just ignored the rules of advertising."
- Scott Bedbury's 1988 Nike brief ("widen the access point").
- W+K London (Honda "Grrr"), W+K's Old Spice era (Kallman/Kuntz).

## Idea fingerprint

- **Chaos is raw material.** "Chaos is the only thing that honestly
  wants you to grow." Don't over-brief or over-systematize an idea's
  early life; protect messy first drafts.
- **Walk in stupid every morning.** Discard category expertise at the
  door — "the minute you think you know… you're dead." Ban "best
  practice" as a starting point.
- **Human truth over product feature.** "Just Do It" was "a sense of
  taking on your life, taking it head on" — write the emotional thesis
  first, attach the product last.
- **Widen the access point.** Brief the emotion of *participation* (joy,
  effort, fear), not elite performance — "we have to stop talking just
  to ourselves" (Bedbury brief).
- **Cultural courage prices in backlash.** Dream Crazy bet on its young
  diverse core knowing it would split the room: 68% awareness in 48h,
  $163M earned media, +31% online sales. A stance with no expected
  sacrifice is not a stance.
- **Fail Harder.** Institutionalize the permission to break things;
  generate options that could plausibly fail, not options that can only
  be approved.
- **Humor is craft.** The Old Spice monologue was written like "a radio
  script" and shot as one continuous practical take — the near-
  clunkiness *is* the charm; committee polish kills it.
- **Speak to the real decision-maker.** Old Spice addressed women (~60%
  of men's body-wash purchases).
- **Steal the line from raw life.** "Just Do It" was adapted from Gary
  Gilmore's last words — hunt taglines in real speech, then compress to
  a 3–4 word imperative that works even if the category disappears.

## Devices (operational)

| Device | Instruction | Canonical proof |
|---|---|---|
| **Anthem arc** | Quiet specific human moment → belief stated in plain declaratives → escalating montage (ordinary strivers first, icons later) → music sparse-to-crescendo → 3–4 word thesis + logo. Product incidental. | Nike "Dream Crazy" |
| **Insider's truth** | Details only a participant would know; test = a real practitioner nods "that's exactly what it feels like." | "Find Your Greatness — Jogger" |
| **Second-person direct address** | Speak straight down the lens to "you", conversational cadence, no cutaways; address the real decision-maker. | Old Spice "Hello, ladies." |
| **Absurdist escalation in one take** | Each beat tops the last (shower → yacht → horse), practical shot, deadpan never acknowledges the absurd. End flattest: "I'm on a horse." | Old Spice (Kuntz, 2010) |
| **Found line from raw life** | Source the tagline outside advertising; compress; validate it works with the category deleted. | "Just Do It" (1988) |
| **Hate-to-change reframe** | Name what everyone hates about the category, destroy it gleefully, product = the act of change. | Honda "Grrr" — "hate something, change something" |
| **Cultural-courage casting** | Cast the person who embodies the contested truth; the casting IS the message. Precondition: brand's documented history must back the stance. | Kaepernick, 2018 |
| **Democratized hero** | Withhold celebrities; give ordinary imperfect bodies the full cinematic anthem treatment. | "Find Your Greatness" (2012) |

## Anti-patterns

```
✗ Corporate-poetry formula (tinkly piano + vague uplift montage — the
  parody-attracting cliché; a form is dead when it spawns parodies)
✗ Leading with features/specs instead of a human truth
✗ Values tourism — a stance with no brand history and nothing sacrificed
✗ Over-polished CG comedy (slickness kills the magic-trick charm)
✗ Expert autopilot ("we've been here before")
✗ Insider jargon that talks only to the purest core
✗ Announcer voice — copy that sounds like advertising
✗ Fake insider detail (slow-mo sweat, trophy lifts — spectator's view)
```

## When to use

- Brand-building moments where feeling beats information; launches,
  repositioning, courage moments.
- Insurance fit: the **insider's truth** of ordinary courage (first
  business, first child, first car) + anthem arc = the emotional case
  for protection. The democratized hero IS the policyholder.

## When NOT to use

- Product-truth demonstration jobs (use `bernbach-ddb`).
- Meme-speed reactive content (use `internet-native-meme`).
- When the client won't back a stance with operations — values tourism
  is worse than silence.
