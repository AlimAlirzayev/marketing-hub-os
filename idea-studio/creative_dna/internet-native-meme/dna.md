# Creative DNA — `internet-native-meme`

**Feed-native cultural fluency.** The Duolingo / Liquid Death / Nutter
Butter / Wendy's / Ryanair school: remixable formats, the brand as a
character with lore, trend-speed shipping, lo-fi over polish — organic
reach earned through cultural fluency instead of media spend. Use when
the goal is native status in the feed, not an ad in the feed.

## Lineage

- **Limor Shifman** (meme scholarship): "following shared pathways for
  meme production is vital for creating a sense of communality."
- **Zaria Parvez** (Duolingo TikTok), **Mike Cessario** (Liquid Death),
  **Michael Corcoran** (Ryanair), Nutter Butter/Dentsu, Wendy's social.

## Idea fingerprint

- **Remixable template, not finished ad.** Publish work whose structure
  others can copy with their own twist; join circulating formats —
  participation IS the community signal.
- **Entertainment company that monetizes via product.** "We're an
  entertainment company that monetizes via beverage" (Cessario). Content
  must survive on entertainment value alone; the product is the
  monetization layer.
- **Speed is structural.** In-house team, no agency loop: "Turnaround
  time is a week. I'm not waiting two months" (Parvez). Duolingo's
  "dead Duo" went concept-to-post in 6 days.
- **One approver.** Ryanair routes every post through a single person —
  "if you tell people what you can't do, you limit their creativity."
- **The audience writes the show.** Mine comments for next week's
  content, then canonize fan theories (Nutter Butter's characters were
  born from audience comments).
- **Own what the internet already mocks about you.** Self-deprecation
  only works on the flaws people actually cite.
- **Calibrated edge.** "If we're not getting some form of cease and
  desist, we're probably not close enough to the line" (Corcoran) —
  provocation as budget substitute, on top of strategy, never instead.
- **Native dialect.** "Don't make ads, make TikToks." Lo-fi handheld
  vertical; polish signals "ad" and gets skipped.
- **Two reconciled personas.** App-Duo (friendly coach) vs feed-Duo
  (unhinged menace) — one character, two contexts, deliberate and stable.
- **Commit to the bit.** Lore compounds; serialized weirdness converts
  viewers into followers.
- **Virality must map to a business metric.** "Whenever we have a viral
  video, we see an uptick in new users" — instrument it, or it's theater.
- **Sass needs a factual spine.** Wendy's roasts are licensed by
  "fresh, never frozen."

## Devices (operational)

| Device | Instruction | Canonical proof |
|---|---|---|
| **Format hijack** | Keep a circulating format's skeleton intact; swap exactly ONE element for the brand's character/truth. Ship within 24–72h of the trend's rise; if you must explain the format, you're late. | Duolingo trend inserts |
| **Brand-as-character** | Replace corporate "we" with one named character (wants, flaws, nemesis, stable voice). Every post = something the character would DO, never an announcement. | Ryanair "Plane Face" — 10M-view videos |
| **Lore threads** | Serialized universe: recurring side characters, unresolved threads, fan theories canonized, payoffs scheduled weeks out. | Nutter Butter "Nutterverse" — 1.6M+ followers |
| **Self-aware cringe** | Make the brand the butt of the EXACT criticisms users already make, exaggerated past the point users would take it. Never invented flaws. | Ryanair fee jokes |
| **Absurdist product placement** | Product = the only stable element in a surreal scenario; delay the "wait, this is official?" reveal until the viewer is invested. | Nutter Butter feed; Liquid Death "murder your thirst" |
| **Mascot jeopardy (event lore)** | Character in serialized peril as a scheduled multi-post event: announce → escalate with evidence → deputize the audience → resolve with a product-mechanic tie-in. | "Duo is Dead" (2025) — mentions +25,560%, #RipDuo 45K+ |
| **Comment-section theater** | Replies are a primary content surface: solicit interactions, answer in-character fast, elevate the best exchanges to posts. Punch at competitors and volunteers, never bystanders. | Wendy's National Roast Day |

## Anti-patterns

```
✗ Lateness — a stale format broadcasts "tourist", not "fluent"; can't ship in the window → skip the trend
✗ Sanitization — committee rewrites that remove the edge; comedy "can't survive in bureaucracy"
✗ Forced slang ("fellow kids") — write a character's voice, never a demographic's
✗ Polish mismatch — ad-grade production in a lo-fi feed marks it as an ad in second one
✗ Roasting without standing — sass with no product truth reads mean and unearned
✗ Dropping the bit — breaking character for a promo; abandoning lore threads
✗ Trend-chasing without brand fit or a measured funnel metric
✗ Edginess as the whole strategy — one viral apology instead of a channel
```

## When to use

- Building an owned social channel into an organic-reach asset; younger
  audiences; low media budgets.
- Insurance fit: careful — regulated category, but **self-aware cringe**
  ("sığorta darıxdırıcıdır, bilirik") + **comment-section theater** are
  usable; a brand character (məs. qayğıkeş amma yorulmaz agent) can
  carry serialized content. Sass needs the factual spine of real claims
  service.

## When NOT to use

- Crisis comms, claims disputes, anything where play reads as disrespect.
- Brands unwilling to grant a single-approver pipeline — sanitization
  kills this DNA at birth.
- One-off campaigns (this is a channel strategy, not a stunt).
