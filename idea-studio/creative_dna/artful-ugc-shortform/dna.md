# Creative DNA — `artful-ugc-shortform`

**Surreal craft in the feed (FOOH & kin).** The 2023–26 genre where UGC-
native delivery meets visible art direction: ONE hyperreal scale/physics
violation on the hero product, anchored in a real recognizable location,
landing as a complete visual statement inside the first 2 seconds. The
visible craft itself is the brand signal ("we can afford taste"). Sibling
of `caring-hand-miniature` — same family, different emotional register
(spectacle/wit vs tenderness).

## Lineage

- **Ian Padgham / Origiful** — the Jacquemus "bags on wheels" solo-artist
  CGI that kicked off the FOOH wave (14.5M+ TikTok / 48M+ Reels, 900%
  one-day search spike, bag became a best seller).
- Maybelline "Sky High" giant lashes on London transport (2023).
- CeraVe "Michael CeraVe" (Ogilvy, Super Bowl LVIII) — deadpan absurd
  celebrity, ~6B earned impressions in 3 weeks before a dollar of paid.
- TikTok Creative Center retention research (the 2-second economics).

## Idea fingerprint

- **One clean violation.** A single scale or physics break on the hero
  product — never two tricks in one clip.
- **The 2-second rule is measurable.** ~71% of users decide in the
  opening seconds; strong intros hold 70%+ retention, weak ones fall
  under 40% and the algorithm never distributes the video.
- **Hyperreal needs a reality anchor.** The stunt is staged in a real,
  recognizable place so convincingly the comments split over "is that
  real?" — the ambiguity IS the share mechanic.
- **Craft flex travels.** Solo-artist authorship and seamless CGI become
  part of the story; visible artistry reads as brand taste.
- **Absurdity must resolve into the product truth.** CeraVe's parody
  lands on "developed with dermatologists, not Michael Cera" — the joke
  is the vehicle for the differentiator, never a replacement for it.
- **Earned-first seeding.** Release ambiguous evidence through creators
  before the polished payoff; the audience arrives already invested.
- **Native delivery beats polish.** Smuggle the art inside a
  creator-style post (phone framing, street POV), not a TV spot.
- **Dual sound/text track.** Sound design hooks the sound-on viewer;
  on-screen text carries the message for the sound-off majority.
- **Series > stunt.** Jacquemus reapplied the same grammar (beach
  Bambino, Seoul crane, handbag freezer) — a repeatable brand world
  compounds; a one-off gag evaporates.

## Devices (operational)

| Device | Instruction | Canonical proof |
|---|---|---|
| **Giant-product-in-city** | Blow the hero product to vehicle/building scale, photoreal, in a real urban landmark. Scale = hook; location = credibility. | Jacquemus Opéra; Maybelline Underground |
| **Impossible-physics loop** | The product behaves as a living thing/machine in a seamless loop that rewards rewatching and "how?" debate. | Bags driving through Paris traffic |
| **Landmark hijack** | Stage on infrastructure everyone knows — reads as news footage, borrows the landmark's fame. | Sky High on London transport |
| **Deadpan absurd celebrity** | Celebrity plays an absurd premise completely straight in luxury-ad grammar; the reveal lands the real claim. | "Michael CeraVe" |
| **Earned-first mystery seeding** | Ambiguous evidence via creators days/weeks before the payoff. Must lean on a REAL existing cultural signal. | CeraVe's Reddit conspiracy |
| **Pattern-interrupt cold open** | Frame ONE is the surreal image — no logo card, no build-up. | Bags open mid-action |
| **Dual-track edit** | Two edits in one file: foley/sound carries the hook, minimal text carries the message. | Genre-wide practice |

## Anti-patterns

```
✗ Slow or logo-first opens (the 2-second window is spent before the idea appears)
✗ Copycat FOOH with no brand-specific idea — the device is SATURATED:
  ~1,872 FOOH videos in 2025, median ~184K views; it only works when the
  violation dramatizes something true about THIS product
✗ Spectacle that buries the product truth (CGI wow, nothing to buy)
✗ Fabricated hype with no existing cultural signal (astroturfing gets called out)
✗ Over-polished TV-spot delivery in a lo-fi feed
✗ A hook that only works with sound on (or only as text)
✗ Broken reality anchor — sloppy CGI/lighting kills the "did that happen?" debate
```

## When to use

- Product launches needing fame fast on small media budgets; brands with
  a visually ownable hero product.
- Insurance fit: the violation must dramatize the CATEGORY truth —
  e.g. a giant umbrella unfolding over a real Bakı block during rain
  (protection made visible), a policy document as a landmark-scale
  shield. Resolve into the real claim (coverage, 3-minute onlayn).

## When NOT to use

- No visually distinctive hero product (the genre needs a subject).
- The idea is "giant product" with no brand-specific truth — saturation
  kills generic spectacle (see anti-patterns).
- Tenderness/care briefs — use `caring-hand-miniature`.
