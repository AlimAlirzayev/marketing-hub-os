# `frameworks.md` — the ideation process (universal)

`creative_dna/` describes how each *tradition* thinks. This file is the
**process that runs under all of them** — how a concept is actually
produced, judged and shaped. `/idea` follows this sequence; the rubric
(`critique/idea_rubric.md`) enforces its rules.

> Grounded, not invented: the process is the Sullivan/Young/Lois craft
> canon plus cross-tradition rules already sourced in `creative_dna/`.

---

## 0. What a Big Idea IS (the bar)

- **Post-it simple.** "It's not a big idea if it doesn't fit on a
  Post-It note" (Sullivan). One sentence carries it, or keep reducing.
- **Unswappable.** If you can swap the logo and the ad still works,
  there is no idea (Sullivan's anti-pattern; Bernbach's product truth).
- **A platform, not an execution.** Test: can you immediately write ten
  more executions from the same sentence? ("I Want My MTV", "You're Not
  You When You're Hungry".)
- **Built to be talked about.** Fame is a design goal, not a side effect
  (Lois: designed "to become a topic of conversation"; see
  `evidence.md` — fame campaigns show the greatest profit growth).

## 1. Truth first (before any ideation)

"Don't put pen to paper until you figure out the truest thing you can
say about a brand" (Sullivan). Write two lines:

- **Product truth** — the verifiable fact worth dramatizing (Bernbach:
  advertising can only *convey* an advantage, never create it).
- **Human truth** — what the audience actually feels (W+K: the emotional
  thesis comes first; "find the emotion — people do things for emotional
  reasons and then rationalize").

No product truth → STOP. Do not polish a lie; push to fix the product
first (Avis made the slogan an internal standard before running it).

## 2. Find the conflict

"Conflict creates story. Without them, you don't have a story"
(Sullivan). Locate the tension: desire vs obstacle, the customer's world
vs the truth, the category convention vs reality. Remember: **"without"
is usually more interesting than "with"** — concept from deprivation and
the problem, not from smiling users.

## 3. Generate in volume — write hot

- "The best way to get a good idea is to get a lot of ideas" (Sullivan).
  Never settle for idea #1.
- **Young's engine:** an idea is "a new combination of old elements",
  and the core skill is *seeing relationships*. Feed it deliberately:
  gather specific material (product, audience) AND general material
  (everything else) before expecting output.
- **Young's 5 steps:** gather → chew (combine from every angle) → drop
  it completely → the flash arrives → shape it against criticism.
- **Walk in stupid** (Wieden): discard category expertise at the door;
  ban "best practice" as a starting point.
- No judging during generation — editing while hot kills the volume the
  cold phase needs.

## 4. Select ruthlessly — edit cold

- Return later in analytical mode. Put everything on the wall; look for
  patterns and collisions.
- **Sacrifice and focus:** one truth per concept. Saying two things =
  saying nothing.
- Run every survivor through `critique/idea_rubric.md`. The kills are
  real (Droga: "idea before execution" — lock the core first, then
  choose the medium it demands).

## 5. Express through a device

Pick the named device from the chosen tradition's `dna.md` (admit-the-
flaw, anthem arc, stunt-with-meaning, format hijack, household-object
metaphor, headline-picture collision…). The device family from the craft
canon:

| Device | Rule | Proof |
|---|---|---|
| **Headline-picture collision** | Headline and visual each incomplete alone; the reader closes the gap — never caption the picture. | VW "Lemon" |
| **Visual twist** | Familiar image, exactly ONE element altered; the alteration is the message. | "Think Small" |
| **Dramatize the benefit** | Stage the benefit as visible drama; the viewer infers the claim. | FedEx fast-talker |
| **Dramatize the problem** | Show life WITHOUT the product at the moment it's needed most. | Got Milk? "Aaron Burr" |
| **Exaggeration anchored to truth** | Inflate the one true claim to an absurd but internally logical extreme; it must collapse back to the literal truth. | Snickers "You're Not You…" |
| **Demonstration as spectacle** | Prove the claim with a real, watchable test pushed to an extreme people want to see. Honest demos only. | Blendtec "Will It Blend?" |
| **Platform idea** | Concept one level above the execution — an idea that generates ideas. | "I Want My MTV" |

## 6. The reader does the last step

Across every tradition the same law: leave the gap the audience closes
themselves (Bernbach: "you cannot sell a man who isn't listening";
Sullivan's collision; caring-hand's unexplained metaphor). An ad that
spells everything out is processed and discarded.

## Anti-patterns (process-level)

```
✗ Laundry-list briefs — two messages weaken each other
✗ Shipping the first decent idea (no wall, no volume)
✗ Editing while generating
✗ Trend-chasing / borrowed interest with no product-truth connection
   (Lois: "every trend is a trap")
✗ Cleverness that could run for any brand in the category
✗ Committee-sanding until the tension is gone
✗ Executing before the concept is locked ("all the tools in the world
   are meaningless without an essential idea" — Lois)
```
